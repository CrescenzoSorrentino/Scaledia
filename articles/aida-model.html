AIDA: The Formula That Still Sells

The 4-step formula to grab attention, spark desire, and drive action.

1 min read
AIDA – Attention, Interest, Desire, Action.
The classic sequence to hook and convert.

At Scaledia, we love timeless strategies that still work. AIDA is one of them. Simple, powerful, and completely ignored by most creators.

What Is AIDA?

AIDA is a 4 step framework used in marketing to guide someone from total stranger to committed buyer.

  • Attention: Grab them fast or lose them.
  • Interest: Give them a reason to keep reading.
  • Desire: Make them want it emotionally.
  • Action: Tell them exactly what to do next.

Concrete Example

Headline: “Discover the trick Duolingo used to triple user growth”

Then you build curiosity, show benefits, and close with a bold CTA.

Why It Matters

Most landing pages miss one of the four. Some catch attention, but never ask for action. Others list benefits but forget to build desire.

If even one step is missing, conversions drop.

Mini Hack by Scaledia

Audit your homepage or last email. Can you clearly label each part as A, I, D, or A? If not, rewrite it until you can.

Example: Hero section = Attention, Features = Interest, Bullets = Desire, CTA = Action.

Final Thoughts

AIDA is not just a copywriting tool. It’s a thinking model. One that keeps your message sharp and your reader moving.

Take 10 minutes. Pick a post. Apply AIDA. Then check your conversions.

Remember: No Attention = No Action.
