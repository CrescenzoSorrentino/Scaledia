Credit Card: Yes or No? Here’s What Ramit Sethi Would Say

Published on April 2025 by Scaledia

Let’s cut through the noise. Some people say credit cards are evil. Others treat them like a money hack. The truth? They’re just a tool. A powerful one if you know how to use it.

The Upside: Why You Might Want One

1. Build Credit and Save Money Long Term

Using a credit card responsibly improves your credit score. That means better rates on loans, mortgages, even your phone contract.

Example: A better score could save you tens of thousands in interest over a mortgage’s lifetime.

2. You're Protected When Things Go Wrong

Ever had a package go missing or a sketchy charge on your statement? Credit cards offer protections debit cards often don’t.

3. Free Money (Seriously)

Rewards programs, cashback, travel points they’re real. If you pay in full every month, these perks are literally free.

Example: Spend €1,000/month with 1.5% cashback = €180/year. That’s dinner out every month, on the house.

The Downside: Why You Should Think Twice

1. Interest Can Wreck You

Carry a balance and you’ll pay interest often 15–25% or more. That €500 item? It might cost you €700 by the time you’re done.

2. You Might Trick Yourself

A credit limit can feel like “free money.” It’s not. If you spend what you don’t have, you’re borrowing from your future.

3. Hidden Fees

Annual fees, foreign transaction charges, late payment penalties they add up fast if you’re not careful.

Smart Credit Card Strategy (Simple Rules)

  • Pay your balance in full every single month.
  • Automate payments so you never forget.
  • Use just one card to keep it simple.
  • Check your statements. Every. Single. Month.

Conclusion: It’s Not About the Card

It’s about you. Your habits. Your discipline. Your awareness.

Bottom line: If you control your card, it’s an asset. If it controls you, it’s a liability.

You don’t need a finance degree. You need a plan. Use credit cards on your terms or don’t use them at all.
